Comparison of CCT to Other Climate Solutions

Carbon capture and storage technologies (CCT) are just one of several technological pathways for addressing climate change. While it plays a critical role in hard-to-abate sectors, it is often compared or contrasted with alternatives such as renewable energy, increasing energy efficiency, or nature-based solutions like reforestation. This article compares CCT to these strategies, not in terms of competition, but complementarity, evaluating costs, scalability, feasibility and climate impact.

1. CCT vs. Renewable Energy (Wind, Solar, Hydro)

Renewables are the foundation of clean electricity. But CCT serves a different function, it doesn't produce clean energy, it cleans up emissions. Therefore, CCT complements renewables. It is not an alternative, especially for cement, steel, and waste-to-energy plants where renewable electricity cannot eliminate CO₂ emissions.

2. CCT vs. Nature-Based Solutions (Forests, Soil, Wetlands)

Nature-based solutions (NBS) are essential tools in the climate portfolio. Reforestation, afforestation, and soil carbon sequestration can store large volumes of CO₂ but have limits. They are vulnerable to wildfires, diseases and droughts. They require vast land areas and they fall victim to the Saturation effect, which states that forests eventually stop absorbing more carbon. 3. CCT vs. Increasing energy efficiency

Improving energy efficiency reduces emissions at the source, often at low cost. However, efficiency can have diminishing returns and cannot eliminate process-based emissions (e.g., from calcination in cement production). A comparison:

Factor Energy Efficiency CCT
Cost per tCO₂ avoided Often very low Moderate to high (dropping over time)
Technical maturity Very high High (in some sectors), growing
Sector coverage All Industrial & power-specific

| In summary, efficiency must be prioritized wherever possible. But CCT addresses residual emissions that cannot be eliminated through efficiency alone.

Conclusion: CCT is a Critical Complement, not a competitor

The climate challenge is massive, and no single solution can deliver net-zero alone. Renewables decarbonize power, efficiency reduces demand, nature buys us time, and CCT cleans up what’s left.

The IPCC’s 6th Assessment Report, the IEA Net-Zero Roadmap, and McKinsey projections all agree: widespread CCT deployment is essential to limit global warming.

In the end, the question isn’t CCT vs. something else. It’s CCT and everything else, in a balanced, realistic portfolio.
